<p><p>The implantable medical textiles market is classified into two main types: non-biodegradable and biodegradable materials. Non-biodegradable textiles are designed for long-term use within the body, offering durability and stability, making them ideal for applications like sutures and mesh. In contrast, biodegradable textiles break down over time, reducing the need for additional surgical removal and minimizing long-term foreign body presence. These materials are suitable for temporary implants and promote natural healing processes, aligning with advancements in tissue engineering and regenerative medicine.</p></p>

<p><p>The Pyrite market consists of three primary types: cuboid crystals, framboids, and anastamozing filaments. Cuboid crystals are characterized by their geometric, block-like appearance, appealing to collectors and jewelry makers. Framboids are spherical aggregates resembling berries, often found in sediments, and are valued for their unique aesthetic. Anastamozing filaments have branching structures, showcasing intricate formations. Each type serves different purposes in ornamental use, industrial applications, and scientific research, contributing to the diverse demand within the mineral market.</p></p>

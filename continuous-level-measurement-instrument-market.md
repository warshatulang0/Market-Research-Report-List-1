<p><p>The continuous level measurement instrument market comprises various types, each with distinct operational principles. Differential Pressure uses pressure differences to determine liquid levels. Guided Wave Radar employs microwave signals along a guided wave for accurate measurement. Non-contact Radar utilizes radar waves to measure levels without physical contact. Ultrasonic sensors emit sound waves to assess distance to the liquid surface. Capacitance measurement detects level changes based on dielectric variations. Other technologies encompass various niche methods suited for specific applications.</p></p>

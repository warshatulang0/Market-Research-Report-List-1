<p><p>Free-Space Optical Communications (FSOC) refers to the transmission of data through the air using light beams, typically via lasers or LEDs, to provide high-speed connectivity over varying distances. FSOC offers a fast and reliable alternative to traditional fiber-optic systems, especially in environments where laying cables is impractical or too costly. <p><p>Free-Space Optical (FSO) Communications encompasses various market types based on wavelength bands. The 1520-1600 nm wavelength FSO system is preferred for longer-range communications, as it experiences reduced scattering and absorption, making it ideal for outdoor and long-distance applications. Other markets include specialized systems that utilize different wavelength bands for niche applications, catering to specific environmental or operational needs.</p></p>

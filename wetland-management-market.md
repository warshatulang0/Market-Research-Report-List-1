<p><p>Wetland management involves various market types for controlling invasive species and maintaining ecosystem health. Chemical treatment, such as herbicides, utilizes chemical solutions to target unwanted vegetation, efficiently reducing competition for native species. Mechanical treatment, on the other hand, involves physical methods, like mowing or excavation, to manage plant growth and restore wetland habitats. Both approaches can be utilized independently or in combination to achieve desired ecological outcomes, contributing to improved biodiversity and water quality in wetland areas.</p></p>

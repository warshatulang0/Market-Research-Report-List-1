<p><p>The bonded ceramic magnet market is characterized by three primary manufacturing processes: extruding, injection molding, and calendaring. Extruding involves forcing a mixed material through a die to create continuous shapes, ideal for long parts. Injection molding combines raw materials in a heated barrel, producing complex, precise shapes quickly and in high volume. Calendering uses rollers to apply pressure and produce thin films or sheets, suitable for applications needing flexible or specific thicknesses. Each method offers unique advantages for various end-uses.</p></p>

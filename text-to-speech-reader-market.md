<p><p>The Text to Speech (TTS) reader market is divided into two main types: online and offline TTS readers. Online TTS readers operate through cloud-based services, requiring an internet connection to convert text to speech, often offering high-quality, customizable voices and regular updates. In contrast, offline TTS readers are installed locally on devices, allowing users to convert text without internet access. This category typically emphasizes privacy and speed but may have limited voice options. Both types cater to diverse user needs and preferences.</p></p>

<p><p>Nuclear reactor coolant pumps are vital for maintaining safe operations in various reactor types. In Pressurized Water Reactors (PWR), coolant pumps circulate water under pressure to prevent boiling. Boiling Water Reactors (BWR) use pumps to circulate water that turns to steam directly within the reactor core. Pressurized Heavy Water Reactors (PHWR) utilize heavy water as a coolant and moderator. Gas-cooled Reactors use gas as a coolant, while Light Water Graphite Reactors combine water and graphite. Fast Neutron Reactors use liquid metal coolants to enhance efficiency.</p></p>
